Daria Gatowska

rogi.design

Daria Gatowska is a 25-year-old graphic designer from New York who ventured out to Arizona to pursue a Graphic Design degree from Arizona State University. Always having had an interest in design and the arts as a means to channel ideas and express personality, having fun with design is crucial to her. Daria strives to push beyond her comfort zone with a direct communication style. She aims to expand her knowledge in book design, poster design, and motion graphics. In her free time, she enjoys learning new things, indulging in boba, and discovering new music to jam and design to.

Gentrification in the United States

Urban Development

Long-standing communities across urban cities in the United States are grappling with transformative changes. Investigating the economic and social forces steering these shifts reveals significant consequences for low-income communities, ranging from the reshaping of cultural identities to the displacement of individuals in these long-standing communities.


The growing impact on the housing market and local businesses has altered affordability and led to a reduction in cultural identity. This often results in the displacement of Black and Hispanic residents, as well as evictions from their homes due to the influx of wealthier individuals into the community.
