The Impact of Social Media on Depression

Mental Health, Social

Growing up in a world that communicates mostly online is affecting younger generations’ mental health, specifically depression. Teens in our society live in a world where communication and entertainment are at the tip of our fingers. Spending more time on social media has been leading to a rise in many teens feeling isolated, experiencing FOMO, and not spending enough time outdoors, leading to depression.


A simple solution would be to spend less time online, but the addiction to social media keeps teens on their phones for an average of 8 hours and 39 minutes. The temporary “high” of activity online keeps teens coming back, while the symptoms of depression rise underneath the surface.
