Social Media Addiction

Mental Health

The typical user spends about 2.5 hours per day on social media. That's 864 hours a year, equal to about one month. One month of constant scrolling, streaming, and deception. About 210 million people globally suffer from social media addiction. Its reach extends to users of all ages, but primarily adolescence. Those susceptible find themselves engrossed in a relentless pursuit of validation through likes and shares, often neglecting real-life responsibilities and face-to-face interactions.


This addiction contributes to a rise in mental health issues, such as anxiety and depression, as users compare their lives to false curated online representations. Beyond individual consequences, social media addiction presents a broader societal challenge, eroding genuine connections.
