Isabelle Clem

clemmy.studio

Isabelle Clem is a multi-disciplinary designer (with a love for print design) first & a self-proclaimed desert rat second. A tinkerer at heart, Isabelle finds the greatest joys in design through exploration processes in finding solutions that she didn’t know she needed. Write something about discovering the intersectionality of design & community that engages unique experiences into visual forms that resonate. While she’s dotty about design, Isabelle finds design as an excuse to revisit her favorite films & extend her postcard collection for inspiration. As of current, Isabelle is earning her BSD in Visual Communication Design at Arizona State University with a specialization in Digital Audiences.

Spatial Mismatch Via Public Transit

Social Infrastructure

For your day to day life, the commute may be abundant in options and opportunities. With nearly 45% of Americans left without access to public transit, the scarce alternatives to transportation leads to disproportionate and detrimental effects on low income and communities of color. As urban planning centralizes and limits destinations on public transit, the barriers to social inclusion and participation from rural cities persist. Whereas opportunities for employment are impacted as the job sprawl (Something about job sprawl is on the rise in the US and it leads to a higher spatial mismatch in AA communities and not white) The basic tenets of equity demands an equal distribution of burdens and benefits throughout a society- Transportation planning can help achieve better social inclusion freeing personal autonomy through redistribution of mobility resources towards communities that are otherwise passed by.
