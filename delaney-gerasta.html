Delaney Gerasta

delaneygerasta.com

Delaney Gerasta is a multidisciplinary designer from the northwest suburbs of Chicago, Illinois. Born into a family of both artists and analysts, Delaney found talents in visual artistry and mathematical thought from a young age. While earning a B.S.D. in Graphic Design with a minor in Statistics, she has developed a method for approaching design challenges with a data-driven mindset, focusing on patterns, research, and analytics. She is enthusiastic to continue utilizing design as a tool to translate complex information and improve experiences across communities through UI/UX design, UX research, or data visualization following her May 2024 graduation.

An Underutilization of Palliative Care

Science, Health, Mental Health

Death is one of the most unifying experiences across all mankind, but the way in which we approach and support end-of-life care does little to maintain quality of life for most patients. Worldwide, less than 20% of people who need palliative care currently receive it; a resource that provides emotional support, spiritual care, and symptom management for patients and their families facing problems associated with a life-threatening illness or condition.


Palliative care is a supportive technique, to not only accompany medical care, but even maximize it to make it more effective across all stages of treatment. It is not a last resort, as many believe it to be. All the evidence points to the consensus that people live better and longer with a unified approach.
