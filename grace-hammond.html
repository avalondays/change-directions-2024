Fentanyl Epidemic Among Adolescents

Social, Education

Fentanyl is a synthetic opioid that is up to 50 times stronger than heroin and 100 times stronger than morphine. Recreational street drugs favored by adolescents have always been a concern, but recently it’s grown to be a public health crisis because they are now often laced with fentanyl. According to the CDC In 2021, fentanyl was involved in 84% of teen overdose deaths. Educated communities are the first line of defense in preventing illicit drug use by teens. There is little to no comprehensive education, prevention or early intervention happening at the federal or state level. For most education happens after tragedy occurs. Real stories, real lives, and real communities are being impacted by this fight against fentanyl.
